Dogs in south America

Dogs are very common pets in South America, and many families have them at home. There are also many street dogs in cities and small towns, and people sometimes feed them. Some countries, like Chile and Argentina, have special programs to help stray dogs. The Peruvian Inca Orchid is a famous dog breed from Peru that has almost no fur. In the Amazon rainforest, some dogs help hunters by tracking animals. Many South Americans love big dogs like German Shepherds and Labradors for protection. In countries like Brazil and Colombia, street dogs often sleep in parks or near food stalls. Some towns have special dog-friendly beaches where people bring their pets to play. In rural areas, dogs help farmers guard their animals and homes. Some South American legends and myths include dogs as loyal and magical creatures. No matter where you go, you will always find friendly dogs living alongside people.
